Welcome to Miku

Edit Page

A filesystem-owned personal wiki that lives inside your own folders — plain Markdown, backlinks, and instant search.

#guide#metaUpdated 2 days ago · 3 min read
Every page is a real .md file on disk. Edit it in Miku or your own editor — the background indexer keeps backlinks and search in sync.

What makes Miku different

  • Your files, your rules. Postgres is a disposable index; your Markdown folder is the source of truth.
  • Wikilinks that just work. Type [[Page]] and the indexer resolves it, backlinks and all.
  • Single-writer safety. Saves are atomic; the file watcher is the only thing that touches the index.

Your first page

Drop a Markdown file into miku/ and the watcher indexes it within ~200ms:

terminal
$ echo "# My First Note" > miku/my-first-note.md
→ indexed my-first-note.md (1 page, 0 links)
“The best wiki is the one that disappears into your own files.” — the whole idea behind Miku.

Ready? Open Markdown & Wikilinks or a Page That Doesn't Exist Yet (dangling links turn pink).
